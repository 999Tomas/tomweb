Planning

1.1 Idea

The door light switch. This device causes the light to automatically turn on when the door opens, and the light is switched off when the door closes. The idea came from the lecturer's advice.
After my research, I decided to generate the Kinetic wireless door switch into my design prototype.
I chose this product because it is without wires or batteries. It's not making a mess in a cupboard, and you don't need to spend extra money by changing batteries. It is pairing with kinetic wireless receiver.
My prototype can be recessed or glued on the desired surfac

1.2 Research

This research helped me understand how to build the device and shows the development process of the device. Additionally, I added my research on how to create an advertisement for the product. The goal was to build the product through video advertising.
I added here a few proven product designs of door switch lights. From these switches, I experimented.
I imagined I was a user. Thus, what I like, what I don't, why I would buy it.
Of all of them, I liked the design of the 'Syndeo door sensor' - it is on the right-hand side in the middle. It is circlular and a soft shape, and visually my brain took me to this design. Also, it is small, and I like the fact that it recessed.
However, I would not buy it, because I don't like it with wires.
Another sensor 'Origins kinetic door sensor' grabbed my attention due to the rounded edges design, and particularly because this product is without wires.
Nowadays, in our houses, we have many items controlled by electricity. Thus, the wires make a mess and people don't like it.
It is great that new ideas and technologies allow us to have fewer wires. However, I would not buy this product because of the big size. In visual, it looks like it could be an obstacle when taking items from the storage.
Therefore, I chose this idea by creating my design prototype, which would be smaller.
Also, I chose to create the switch, because it is relevant. The user likes automatic things, as there is no need to press or push - anything. It is also easy to install without needing a specialist.

1.3 Exploring Device Design: A Comparative Analysis

I wanted to get a better idea of what shape to design a device the user will buy.
From my research, I found that rounded edges, circle and squircle are friendly and inviting for our eyes, so I purchased a few design devices: two kinetic and one with the wires.
I also wanted to see what mechanical components were needed, and how these devices work.
I was initially planning to print my design prototype, but I had to cancel this stage, because of lack of time.
From all of them, the circle shape device attracts my attention. I would buy it as a user if it did not have wires.
The 'Quinetic' switch: - I do not like the square shape, sharp edges, and for the cabinet mounted on to the surface, it is too big.
The squircle as I mentioned in the research stage, is a nice smooth design, but also too big.
Therefore, I have now got my design vision, and I know what mechanical components are inside. This means I can animate my prototype in 3D and see how it will work.

1.4 Sketches

I decided my device prototype design will be semicylinder form, and a small size. It can be recessed into the cabinet or only adhere to the surface.
I did not want any screws for my model. I want the installation to be straightforward for users. Those users who don't like to use a drill can affix it onto the surface.
The front face will be flat with a hemisphere form head, and the head with compression function.
When the light is switched off with the door closed, this will compress the switch. When the door opens, the head will rebound, switching the light on.
On the model sides, it will be fixators and the model will fix into the recessed area.

Model Development

2.1 Base Mesh Creation

The base mesh I created from a primitive cylinder. I deleted front, back and half cylinder faces and then filled. Then I separated the front face for the model head. Also, I slightly scaled up. Afterwards, I used a solidify modifier for both meshes. To make a hemisphere form head, I used a sphere mesh. Firstly, I rotated, and then I made a hole on the front face by using a boolean modifier with a sphere mesh. I needed this cavity for the head compressing function. When the head is pushed, it can go through this cavity. Then I made face extrusions inward from both body mesh sides, and finally by extruding edges I created fixators. The mechanical components were created from the base mesh by duplicating, extruding and scaling inward faces.

2.2 Texturing

Texture was created using Principled BSDF. I added a colour base and by making it slightly glossy, I reduced the roughness value.

2.1 Billboard

I wanted to make an advert for my product prototype in the outdoor scene. So I came up with the idea to place it on Billboards. Thus, I first made my prototype model renders, then I made a cupboard and placed it into a scene with lights. Afterwards, I made a render with one prototype which adheres onto the surface, and another render when the model recessed into the cabinet. Then I created an advertisement by using all these renders in Ps. Lastly, I created a Billboard model. For the environment, I used the city with many buildings, and to make it more interesting, I rendered from the monorail. The idea was that people travelling by monorail would be looking at the advert. To save my time, I used this scene from my personal project, which I created last year.

2.2 Video Advertising

I created this video by imagining it for users. I made the video with white background for the explanation and installation. So The that my product would stand out. In my research, I found that most product video ads are on a completely white background. Also in the white background, the buyer's attention is drawn to the product. The customers are not getting distracted. Also, I experimented with the scenes where I'm displaying how it is used. For that scene, I created a kitchen room using real life textures.
