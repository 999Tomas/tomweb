2.1 Chest Base Modelling

Firstly, I started modelling a base. I have used the cube primitive. The form was scaled into x-axis, so that the model form would be the right proportion. Then on edit mode, I added two loop cuts in z-axis and scaled into x-axis. The same process I did by adding loop cuts in x-axis and scaling it onto z-axis. It increased the poly count in my mesh. These faces were required for the extrusions. The model's capacity was created by extruding downward top mid-surface. This model needed a recess in all sides mid-faces. I did it by extrusion inward. By following a reference, I made extrusions outward on all top and bottom corner faces.

2.2 Lid Modelling

I duplicated the top faces from my base mesh and separated it. I made the lid bottom part by extruding this mesh upward. The next step is the top lid part. The middle top edges of the bottom lid mesh were copied and scaled outward. Then I separated it. The reason I made this action is that I will solidify this mesh later. The bottom part already has thickness. In that case, I did not need additional polygons for this part. As this model's main goal is a low poly count. Next, I did the height. The edges were extruded upward and filled in. I then rounded the top edges using the bevel tool (shortcut ctrl+b) on the top length edges. To follow my reference, I needed to increase the poly count for more extrusion operations. I added two loop cuts in bevelled surface and inserted faces on both mesh sides. Afterwards, I extruded the top mid-faces and both sides's centre faces inward. My mesh has been thickened by applying the solidify modifier. To add additional detail to my mesh, I pushed the top centre faces upward of both sides. Then I joined the bottom part with the top.

2.3 Meshes Deformation
Lid

To make my model more interesting, I deformed it. For the cover mesh, I randomly chose some vertices, and by grab tool it was moved to different directions. Additionally, for the lid bottom front face, I used a knife cut tool. I sliced a few times, and then by grab tool moved up and down and a bit inward.

Base

I added a few more loop cuts. Then I transformed my mesh by scaling inward in x-axis with proportional editing. Firstly, by selecting both sides edge pieces and afterwards both bottom corner faces.

Shade Smooth

Last step, I shaded smooth my both mesh parts. This smoothing feature made my model surfaces appear smooth.

2.4 Lock Modelling

This part I have used the primitive cube. I made the right size by scaling it firstly in x-axis and then in y-axis both inward. Then I bevelled the top sides edges. I needed a couple of polygons on my front face. However, the bevel tool made my front and back faces N-gon. Thus, on these faces, I could not add any loop cuts. To sort out this issue, I needed quads. I made quads in both N-gon sides by connecting the vertex path. Then I added a loop cut. Before extrusion operations, I scaled the bottom and mid-vertices inward in x-axis by proportional editing. I followed my reference, thus the next act was extrusions. Firstly, I extruded two top faces. Then on the extruded bottom face, I added one more loop cut. This face I also pushed out. After that, this last extrusion, the bottom and top edges I bevelled.

2.5 Lock Hole

This is a mesh for the Boolean modifier. I used the cylinder mesh. It was scaled, and then I extruded three of the bottom mid-faces downward. I added one loop cut in this extrusion centre face. It added two vertexes in the centre, so I transformed the form by moving these vertices downward with proportional editing. It was moved down till I got the right form, as it looks like a lock hole. Then I made the first part of my mesh visible. I selected it and added Boolean modifier. Next step, I selected the target mesh with eyedropper. As a result, this modifier cut my main mesh with this lock hole mesh by the difference operation. Thus, I got a hole in my mesh. However, I had to apply this modifier. Otherwise, when I moved, the main mesh had no cutter hole. Then, I deleted the lock hole mesh, since it was no longer necessary.

2.6 Handle Modelling

The handle model was made from a cube and a curve. I have used the cube for the top part. In this part, I do not need to perform many acts. The only thing I changed was the cube proportions using the scale tool. A curve was used to create the finger pull part. I scaled the curve in the x-axis in the right proportion. I then grabbed the vertices at both ends and scaled inward. There are five vertices on the curve. So, I grabbed the centre curve downward and the last two vertices upward until I could make one straight line with the end vertexes. I then clicked on the object properties data and increased the depth of the bevel to add geometry. The curve is not a mesh. I then converted it to mesh and joined it with the top part. In my last act, my model had two handles, so I used a mirror modifier. However, I had to apply the location of the model before that.

2.7 Hardware Modelling

This part was made from a cylinder primitive with seven vertices. It was scaled whole and then in y-axis. According to my reference, I needed to copy this mesh many times.
Thus, I used the mirror modifier by x-axis and y-axis. Afterwards, I duplicated it a few times and moved it upward. Lastly, I duplicated a single mesh and placed it in the right place for the top rounded surface. In the next step, I selected my cylinder mesh and added the array modifier. Array modifier creates multiple copies with desired offsets. In my case, my model top surface is rounded, so I needed the empty. The empty is my object offset. It allowed rotation or scalability. This act was completed by increasing the count of the array modifier. Once I ticked object offset, I clicked on my empty with the eyedropper. In the end, I rotated my mesh with empty as needed.

Texturing

I chose texture painting for all chest model metal parts. The lock, handle, the cylinder shape decors. Also, all extrusions outward on a chest base with a cover mesh were painted.
All the other parts on my main mesh and cover were wooden. The wood was created using PBR (Physically Based Render) texture. It was chosen because it gave a slight realism of my model.

1.7 UVM & PBR Textures with Texture Painting

To start using texture painting, I unwrapped my mesh on edit mode by pressing shortcut A (selected all). I have used a smart UV project, unwrapping automatic. It took my 3D mesh into a 2D map. This is a link where texture paints. Thus, I can place a texture onto that object.
Afterwards, by clicking shortcut (Ctrl+Tab), the object interactions mode appears. I chose the texture painting tab, and then onto active tool settings, I chose a single image and created a new texture. I left the resolution 1024, it is enough for my simple low poly model. Also, the animation can be faster by using this resolution. I changed the colour to brown. It is the main colour on the chest metal part.

Metal

Finally, I started shading. On the final image, it could be seen that my cover metal part is lighter than the main model part. I painted separately, and then it was hard to remember all the shading steps to get the same result. I tried to hide it by scene lightning, but it didn't help much in a transparent background. In the animation scene, in some angles it looks good. The scene is dark shade, and also their smoke effect, volumetric light, souls (particles) models with blue emissive materials does not allow me to see my mistake.

Wooden

The wooden part was made with PBR texture.
Firstly, I added a coloRamp node and a few colours. I did a simple procedural shader with only one Musgrave texture generator. Musgrave generator was doubled and stacked together.
Then I selected the duplicated Musgrave texture and pressed a shortcut Ctrl+T. It added a mapping node with a texture coordinate node. I have changed the connections from generated to object. It will be easier to apply to other models, and the texture will look similar. Then I transformed the pattern by scaling.
By default, the all axis have a value 1. I wanted the patten to be horizontal lines. Thus, I reduced the amount of scale on x-axis and y-axis by 0.1, and increased the amount on z-axis by 2.00.

Afterwards, I added one more colorRamp node and a bump node. I connected the coloRamp to the bump height, and a bump node to P BSDF normal. To get the result like a groove pattern. I clicked on bump invert. Also, the bump strength has a value 1 too high. So, I reduced until my texture was fine-looking. My texture looks shiny.

I fixed that. When I added another coloRamp and connected to the Musgrave texture, and the coloRamp colour node connected to principled BSDF roughness. Lastly, I swapped the black and white stops in coloRamp. This fixed the problem.

Animation

Model Animation

Firstly, I made the chest model animation. I selected the lid and on edit mode chose bottom back start and end edges. It was allocated the 3D cursor between these edges. This action has been done because the lid swings on this point.
Then I hit 'I' on item rotation in transform panel, and on y-axis I added 360 degrees , and again I hit 'I'.

Turntable Animation

I was adding empty. Then I selected the model and made the empty active (yellow) and hit shortcut Ctrl+p for parented.
Then on frame one in the view I hit 'I'. It added a key. Afterwards, I moved my timeline to the last frame, and on the transform panel I changed the Z rotation to 360, and I hit 'I' again with my cursor over that.
By default, the animation is on bezier. The speed was not the same on the end, slowing down. Thus, I fixed that by changing interpolation type to linear by hitting the shortcut Ctrl+t with the cursor on timeline.
So then my low poly model has sculpted details.

Scene Animation

I added the empty and then selected my camera, to which I was added a constraint track to and targeted to the empty. Next step, I selected the camera, and then hit I in the viewport appeared menu. I chose a location, and I have a first key on frame 1 in the timeline. Then I left-clicked and dragged the top bar on the 250 frames. Thus, I slightly moved the camera and hit 'I' again. The movement action done.
The next part on physics properties. I changed the smoke time frame start and the particles as well. They will start only when my chest cover starts opening. Also, the smoke simulation and particles will stop earlier before my lid closes.
The particles which represent my soul's speed is too high. It looks weird and it's not what I wanted to create. This is my mistake, by rushing and a lack of settings knowledge.
