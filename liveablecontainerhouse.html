Preparation

1 Introduction to Project

I have always been fascinated by the art and science of architecture, the way it blends form and function to create structures that not only serve a purpose, but also leave a lasting impression on those who experience them.

This semester, I have delved into architecture. I wanted to learn about the intricate process of creating these structures. To understand the various stages involved in the architectural process, from conceptualisation and design to construction and implementation.

One of my main goals for this semester was to improve my CAD skills. CAD software is an essential tool in architecture, and I wanted to better understand its capabilities and how to use it effectively.

This project I was attracted to developing is the brief the “Caring Culture.”
Through this project, I hope to improve the lives of older adults by providing them with a comfortable and affordable home, as well as a supportive community where they can thrive and enjoy their golden years. 'New Beginning' is not just a physical structure, but a symbol of hope and a better quality of life for our seniors. With this project, we can create a caring culture where older adults are valued and supported, and their contributions to society are recognised and appreciated. It is a small step towards a bigger goal of creating a society where everyone, regardless of age, can lead a fulfilling and dignified life.

The idea is a mobile container home that could be easily transported and set up in other community locations.
The design of the container house is simple and practical, with a focus on accessibility and comfort for older adults. The interior is spacious and open, with wide doorways to accommodate mobility aids.
Outside, the foldable porch provides space for relaxation and enjoying the surrounding nature. The green roof not only adds to the aesthetic appeal of the house, but also helps with insulation and reducing energy costs.
But "New Beginning" is not just about providing affordable and accessible housing for older adults. It also aims to create a sense of community and support for residents. The retirement container houses are located close together, allowing social interactions and shared activities among the residents. There is also a community centre that offers various services, such as health check-ups, social events, and educational programs.
The container house model incorporates several unique features, one of which is a foldable porch. This porch provides a versatile outdoor space that can be easily expanded.

My model boasts two terraces, each with sliding WPC frame construction windows. These terraces are the perfect place to unwind and take in the surrounding views. With these unique features, my model offers a perfect balance of indoor comfort and outdoor connection, making it an ideal living space for seniors.

The video shows the seamless process of how these parts can be extended, and how the result looks with added textures. The realistic animation and final render give a glimpse into how the model would look in real life, providing a clear understanding of the design and its potential.

1.1 Why Containers?

Container houses can be customised and designed to fit the specific needs and preferences of the occupant. This is especially helpful for seniors who may have mobility or accessibility issues. They can have a home tailored to their needs, making it easier for them to navigate and live comfortably.

Apart from the practical benefits, container houses also have a unique and modern aesthetic. They can be designed in various styles and look like traditional houses. Container houses offer a sustainable, affordable, and customizable housing option that can greatly improve the quality of life for seniors. They are a solution to the increasing cost of living and the growing concern for the environment.

1.2 Primary Target Audience Research | Paper Survey

Undertaking this survey was a challenging task for me, especially since my target audience was older adults.
As I delved into my research, I came to the realisation that many individuals in this age group do not use the internet, making it difficult for me to conduct an online survey. With my tutor's guidance, I decided to take to the streets to gather my primary research. However, I soon discovered that this group of individuals were not keen on participating in surveys. While they were friendly and willing to engage in conversation, they were reluctant to answer my questionnaire.
I had to come up with an alternative plan, I interviewed people in the library in the supermarket and at the railway station. In total, I managed to gather 23 participants, which was a significant accomplishment considering the challenges I faced.
This survey was not only time-consuming, but also required a lot of effort to convince older adults to participate. However, it was worth it, as their opinions and perspectives are crucial in my research. Overall, this survey was a valuable experience that not only provided me with valuable data for my research, but also deepened my understanding of the needs and behaviours of the senior citizens.

I asked their opinions, what they thought about container houses, whether they felt it is a feasible option for living in. If they could see themselves living in one. What they liked and disliked about the mock up test I had created.

[1.2.1] Statistics show 69% of people believe a container house is suitable for living in, while 22% are unsure and 9% do not think it is a viable option. With a high percentage of them thinking positively about container houses, it is truly gratifying. This statistic is proof that perhaps the older generation is more open-minded and willing to consider alternative options for their housing needs.

[1.2.2] The responses to the Display about the idea of living in a container house included: 44% of respondents expressed openness to the idea, while 52% stated they were gravitating towards a 'yes' response. The numbers indicate that most seniors are intrigued by the idea of living in a container house.
From my sample it appears to prove that they are open to the idea of living in a container house.

[1.2.3] From the Display data gathered from the participants. It is clear that most of them are interested in homes with 2-3 bedrooms.
This finding is crucial, as it directly impacts on the design and development of the one-bedroom container house prototype I have been working on. Knowing that my target audience prefer homes with 2-3 bedrooms, I can now focus on creating a design that caters to their needs and preferences.

The last two questions that were multiple choice eliciting responses in my survey have been incredibly beneficial in helping me come up with ideas for my new container house prototype.
I gained valuable insights into what my target audience dislikes about my primary mock up design.

[1.2.4] The results showed 86% of my target audience found the house too small, while an overwhelming 91% did not like the concept of a roof terrace. Additionally, 52% of the participants disliked the colours used in the design. These findings have given me valuable insight into areas that require more attention and improvement in my model. It has also helped me understand the preferences and expectations of my target audience, allowing me to make necessary changes and modifications to my prototype.

[1.2.5] The final question in my research focused on the participants' wishes and preferences in housing.
100% of my participants agreed with two key factors: the importance of low price and energy efficiency in their ideal home. This shows a clear trend towards more practical and sustainable living choices.
Additionally, 96% of participants expressed a desire for the threshold at the entrance to be level, with no steps, making it easier for individuals with mobility issues or disabilities to enter and exit the home.
The majority, 91%, also emphasised the importance of having an accessible bathroom and incorporating renewable energy sources into the home.

Another aspect that was highly valued by 91% of the participants was the need for ample space and flexibility in their ideal home.
It was also interesting to note that a green environment was important to 87% of participants, further highlighting the growing awareness and importance of environmental sustainability.
Additionally, 83% of the participants expressed a need for sufficient storage space and a porch, which are both practical and desirable features in a home.

Hearing these responses from my participants was enlightening, as many of these factors had not crossed my mind during the initial stages of my research. It shows the importance of considering the perspectives and preferences of those who will live in new homes when designing and building them. Overall, the unanimous wishes and desires of my participants regarding housing serve as valuable insight and highlight the need for practicality, sustainability, and inclusivity in housing design.

1.4 Ideate

The installation of ceiling heating. This heating system not only provides thermal comfort, but also helps save energy costs.
In addition to the main living space, the container house will also have a manually extendable porch that can be mounted on the container wall. This porch will provide a comfortable outdoor seating area for the residents, allowing them to enjoy the front of their home and interact with their neighbours.

The offset configuration of the containers also allows the creation of two terraces. One will be located off the master bedroom and another from the dining area. It provides a perfect spot for relaxing and enjoying the surrounding nature.

To make the container house stand out and have a unique design, the idea is to incorporate a sliding WPC planks frame construction with narrow windows. This design not only adds visual interest, but also serves a functional purpose. The frame will function as a roof for the terraces, providing protection from the rain, while the vertical construction will block the strong south wind. The other open sides of the terraces allow for a connection to the environment and provide a sense of openness and freedom.

Next part is a roof. I chose a green shed type of roof.
This type of roof has a single slope, making it easy to build and maintain. Additionally, the slope of the roof allows rainwater and snow to easily run off, reducing the risk of leaks and damage.

A green roof, also known as a living roof, is a roof partially or completely covered with vegetation. A green roof is an environmentally friendly, cost-effective, and visually appealing option for the roof of a container house. It has numerous benefits, such as energy efficiency, roof protection, flood prevention, and biodiversity support. It is a great way to transform a simple container house into a sustainable and beautiful home.

Solar panels are a great option for a container house because they are low maintenance, have a long lifespan, and can save seniors energy costs.

By incorporating a living shed roof with a solar panel system, the container house will not only have a unique and visually appealing design but will also stand out as a symbol to be energy-efficient and environmentally friendly.

1.9 Foldable Porch

One of the most innovative and practical features of this container home design is the folding porch. This concept takes into consideration the occupants needs, whether it be travel, or a lifestyle. The porch is designed to be easily transported along with the rest of the container home and won't need to be dismantled every time. This will save time and effort, but also adds to the convenience and functionality of the container home.

The porch is attached to the front of the container home and can be easily folded up or down.
The views on the right side of the cad drawing show how the porch can be unfolded outwards.
The non-unfolded elevations and top views on the left side of the cad drawing, demonstrates how the porch is in its folded down position. I gave detailed cad drawings of how it would look when it was deliverable or transportable.

The porch folding mechanism is a series of hinges that allow easy folding and unfolding. These hinges are strategically placed to ensure stability and durability, while also allowing smooth and effortless movement

One of the major advantages of this folding porch design is its ease of use. The simple folding mechanism requires minimal physical effort, it would be accessible for those with mobility issues or limited strength.

I also added the animation below to the cad drawing, which gives a visual representation of how the porch would be easily unfolded.

The folding porch for the container home will not only add to the design and aesthetic of the home but will also be useful for those seniors who frequently want to change community locations.

2.1 Choosing Colour Palette

My container house model is a unique and modern structure that exudes warmth and tranquillity with its dominant colours of cherry wood and light shade of yellow. These hues create a harmonious and inviting atmosphere that is both stylish and comforting. The cherry wood colour, with its deep and rich tones, gives a sense of elegance and sophistication to the exterior of the house.

The light shade of yellow was chosen, to complement the cherry wood and add a touch of brightness and positivity to the overall design. Yellow is often associated with happiness and joy, and by incorporating it into the exterior of the container house, it conveys optimism and positivity. This is especially important for seniors, who may transition into a new phase of their lives and need a boost of energy and enthusiasm.
The other three specific colours were generated using the Colormind.io scheme generator. The first colour, sandstone, was chosen for the decking, entrance door.
The second colour, alligator skin, was selected for the canopy support steel component.

The third colour, olive green, was chosen for the cladding of the roof sides.
Together, all these colours create a well-balanced and cohesive colour scheme for this container house model.

Overall, the exterior of this container house is designed to convey a sense of warmth, optimism, and joy. It is a place where seniors can feel comfortable, happy, and at peace.
