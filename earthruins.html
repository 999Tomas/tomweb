Modelling Character

This video shows how I created a penguin character. It is a screenshots and clay rendering. I then combined them and made a video.

Base

I added a reference in the background. It helps create a form faster.
Then I started modelling from the side view with a cube primitive.
The base form I did by extrusions: the bottom faces downward a few times and I moved the side vertices outward till I fit on my reference in the background.

Tail

Afterwards, I created the tail. I made it by selecting the second bottom face of the front view. Then I inserted an extra face and moved downward. Lastly, I extruded two times and moved vertices inward.

Head

The head part I created by extruding the top faces a few times and moving vertices inward. Then slightly rotating the top faces and scaled inward, and then again extruded the top faces. These actions I repeated a few times until I got the beak.

Legs

I inserted an extra face on the bottom polygon, moved downward and extruded a few times. Then the end face scaled inward and again extruded a few times.

Feet

I sliced the front face a few times and extruded into the y-axis by selecting individual faces and scaled inward. Then added a loop cut in the middle, and the top edges moved downward.

Nails

This part I created by inserting on the toes extra faces slightly moved into y-axis, and again inserted extra faces, rotated and I extruded a few times, scaled inward, rotated and again scaled inward.

Flipper

I inserted an extra face on the side. Then extruded outward and moved down, scaled the end faces inward, and again extruded and moved down and repeated. Next, by selecting edges, I moved in -y-axis and on the end a bit inward. Lastly, I added a couple of loop cuts and the first loop cuts I slightly scaled inward.

Eyes

I inserted an extra face between the beak and head area. Then I clicked the right mouse on the appeared menu loop tools, selected the circle, and scaled it down. I rotated and pushed it out a bit.

Beak

Then I improved form to the beak on the front view by moving vertices inward. To make the beak open, I selected the first front middle edges until I had half of the middle beak and clicked the shortcut (V). This act makes a gap in the beak. Then I slightly moved these edges downward. Afterwards, I selected the top edges one and together moved inward. Lastly, I merged these edges. So, I created the oral cavity.

High Poly to Low Poly

I wanted to create a bumpy skin texture for a penguin. So, to have a high-resolution mesh, I did the following process. First, I duplicated the low poly model, unwrapped it to a 2D image, and hid it. I have used a multi-resolution modifier, subdivided 5 times. This smooth surface allowed me to get a better result by sculpting Then I went to sculpt mode. I have added alpha skin texture. This is my result. As a final step, I selected a high poly mesh and then a low poly mesh and baked them. So, then my low poly model has sculpted details.

Stylized Character

Hair

It was made from the head top surfaces. I selected, copied and separated. Then I went back to edit mode, extruded up this mesh and by grab tool and more extrusions stylized. Finally, I shaded smooth and added subdivision modifier by level 1.

Jacket

I copied and separated the surface from the penguin body. Then on edit mode, I selected a vertical middle edge, and by using shortcut (V) I made a seam and moved slightly by creating a bigger gap between edges. Then by selecting the top jacket edges, I scaled it outward. I have made a wider neck area. Then I made a jacket thickness by adding the solidify modifier. Afterwards, I made a couple of changes on the top shoulder edges and sleeve by moving it by proportional editing. Finally, I have created a simple button from the cylinder and duplicated it.

Goggles

I used a plane scaled down. Then added loop cut and deleted half. Added again loop cut and scaled inward middle edges and added a subdivision modifier. I increased the level 2. Then I added the mirror modifier and applied it. Afterwards I inserted extra faces and used solidify modifier and applied it. Because the next step I used the extrusion around to get more thickness only for frames. Then an applied subdivision modifier and by proportional editing scaled in z-axis the middle edges and moved up. Then inserted faces again in glasses area. It will make visible edges between frame and glasses area and lastly, I added simple deform modifier and by added empty I bend my goggles as I needed.

LET'S CLOTHES

Texturing

I used a simple texture for Dr. Pepi's hair, eyebrow and jacket buttons. I have used a Principled BSDF texture by adding a base colour in blender. All other parts of the texture were created in Adobe 3D Substance Painter.
I've never used this software before so it was a challenge. I chose it because was it interesting to me. Lack of knowledge of this software made getting a cartoonish texture difficult, so the results appear realistic.

Body

For the body I have created alpha channel skin texture with black and white colours.

Jacket

It was created using a fabric texture and I added a colour layer. I have used orange colour as our game logo.
I don’t like my jacket's front edges, it’s look like been stretched. After feedback, I decided to improve my model by adding some more polys. Therefore, subdivision impacted my texture. I did not have time to repeat the texture process, as presentations were impending.

Goggles

The goggles frames, I stylised to look bumpy as It was on the sketches. I used a metal panel texture. Then I scaled it so that the texture would be more visible. Afterwards, I have added a colour layer, the same as the jacket.
Lastly, I added a glass material with purplish colour.

Hair & Eyelashes

I created a simple procedural texture by adding base colour.

Scene Creation

I wanted my Dr. Pepi to be in a snow environment. The purpose was to keep it simple and quick, with the least amount of stuff in the scene. So I added a plain to the penguin place and scaled it along the x axis. I then added a snow procedural texture that I created by following a tutorial.
Next, I added a landscape from blender add-ons. This mesh has been scaled a few times, and then I duplicated it for the far away background. I then positioned from the camera view.
I added the same snow texture by copying it from the plane, using the shortcut (Ctrl+L).
In this scene, I have used a traditional three-point light.
The last step was to blur the background. I wanted the camera focus to be just on my main mesh. As a result, I selected the camera, checked the depth of field, and increased the f-stop.

Animation

I did not need to create all this animation for our game. I made these to learn and improve my animation skills.

Dr. Pepi walking

First, I pressed the auto key on timeline. It fixed my movements automatically. Then on pose mode, I started movements by selecting bones. First move pose the one side leg, I grabbed down. Then I chose the main bone and bent a body to the right side, and then I clicked the left side leg bone and slightly grabbed up. First pose done.
Then I selected all bones shortcut (A) hit 'I' and on the appeared menu I inserted location, rotation & scale.
Next, I dragged my timeline on frame 10 and repeated the process on the opposite side. I also made some neck and head movement, and again selected all bones and hit 'I', location, rotation & scale.
Then I drag my timeline to frame 100. Penguin must be on frame 100. So, I pressed G and grabbed the penguin to frame 100. On this frame, Dr. Pepi jumps up. So, I selected all bones and grabbed up and again I hit 'I' and location, rotation & scale.
Then I move a timeline on 110 and grabed penguin back to down, and hit 'I' and location, rotation & scale.
Next pose on 120 frame I had the penguin looking to the camera. I selected neck bone and slightly rotating to the camera, and hit 'I' location, rotation & scale. Then grab timeline on 150, and on that frame, I made a penguin wave by moving a hand bone up, and again I hit 'I', location, rotation & scale.
Then I went back to the beginning and repeated the walking movements and copied the keys till frame 100.

Work Light

I used two cameras for this animation. The first one till frame hundred. I added a curve circle. Then added a camera and on active camera I added constraint follow path and targeted to curve. Then I chose the correct axis and ticked follow curve and pressed animate path. Afterwards, on active camera I added another constraint track to and cliked on target to. So when the camera moves around, it always focuses on the work light model.
From frame 100, my second camera was active. It was tracked to the model and I added a key by hitting 'I' on the start panel. Then I moved my timeline bar to frame 200, and then I moved the camera by increasing the amount on y location till I needed, and hit 'I' again. From frame 200, my work light started rotating, and also I added a light key. The light switch was on when the work light head starts rotating. I also added a few more keys on light colour. It changes colours four times between 20 frames. I also animated the light strenght.

Solar Panel

I was adding empty. Then I selected the model and made the empty active (yellow) and hit shortcut (Ctrl+p) for parented. Then on frame one in the view I hit 'I' and selected location, rotation. It added a key. Afterwards, I moved my timeline to the last frame, and on the transform panel I changed the Z rotation to 360, and I hit 'I' again with my cursor over that. By default, the animation is on bezier. The speed was not the same on the end, slowing down. Thus, I fixed that by changing interpolation type to linear by selecting the shortcut (Ctrl+t) with the cursor on timeline.
